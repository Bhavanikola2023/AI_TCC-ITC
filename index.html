AI Functions Used by Faculty

72% of faculty use AI for content generation, making it the most popular function. This includes quiz creation, slide generation, and other content automation tasks.

Faculty usage time is distributed across categories, with 33.3% spending minimal time (0-30 min) and 22.2% being power users (121+ min).

Key Insights & Recommendations

While 94.4% of faculty include AI policies in their syllabi, only 61.1% have redesigned assignments with AI literacy components. This suggests a gap between policy adoption and practical implementation.

Recommendation:

Provide targeted workshops focused on practical assignment redesign with concrete examples and templates.

55.6% of faculty report lower workload/stress due to AI tools, indicating these technologies are helping reduce administrative burden.

Recommendation:

Showcase workload-reducing use cases from successful faculty adopters to encourage broader adoption.

The moderate positive correlation (0.51) between professional development hours and faculty confidence highlights the importance of continued training investments.

Recommendation:

Expand professional development opportunities with tiered training paths based on faculty experience levels.

77.8% of faculty have not engaged in cross-college AI collaborations, representing an untapped opportunity for interdisciplinary work.

Recommendation:

Create a cross-college AI teaching innovation group to foster collaboration and interdisciplinary applications.
